01 — About

Applied Mathematics undergraduate at Noakhali Science and Technology University (NSTU), Bangladesh. My work sits at the intersection of cognitive science, information geometry, and game theory — building theoretical frameworks that are both mathematically rigorous and empirically motivated.

I develop and refine original frameworks including Top-K Cognitive Compression (TKC) and Elastic Truth Geometry (ETG), alongside applied projects like DRIVNET — a geo-social platform for truck drivers with Bayesian reputation scoring and Markov career modelling. I believe mathematics is a lens for understanding cognition, coordination, and the structure of truth itself.

Previously served as admin of the Linux Users in Bangladesh group (2019). Open-source advocate, Linux enthusiast, and lifelong learner operating at the frontier of applied mathematics and computational theory.

05 — Research & Projects
Theoretical Framework
Elastic Truth Geometry (ETG)
Models epistemic states as geometric structures S = (X, F, R, C, T). Opponents rotate axes without lying: S′ = Rθ(X). Introduces operator algebra {Ω_up, Ω_expand, Ω_meta, Ω_compress, Ω_down} and Epistemic Stability Index ESI = β/α. Targets arXiv submission.
Cognitive Science
Top-K Cognitive Compression (TKC)
Information-theoretic model of cognition as bounded compression. Models how minds prioritize and discard information under cognitive load. Unified ETG-K paper in preparation.
Applied Platform
DRIVNET
Geo-social platform for truck drivers in Bangladesh. Features Bayesian reputation scoring, Markov career modelling, and a data intelligence layer for decentralized coordination.
Game Theory
Spatial Congestion Signals
Real-time signal design for decentralized coordination in spatial congestion games. Target venues: WINE, SAGT, EC, GEB. Multiple rounds of novelty assessment and reference verification completed.
